A short one — three items today. Two on benchmark plumbing (the reason
this matters for the agentic-economy beat) and one on a Swiss
broadcasting bill we have not seen any English-language coverage of yet.

## MMLU has officially saturated; LMArena is now the only signal at the top

The latest [Llama-5 70B card](https://example.com/llama5-card) ships with
89.4% on MMLU, ~1.2 points behind the current frontier and inside the
typical run-to-run noise band. That's the third frontier release in five
months whose MMLU number lands inside the noise of the previous best.
The benchmark is done.

What this means in practice: the few labs still publishing MMLU as a
leaderboard claim are either drafting model cards on autopilot or
optimising for the headline. Third-party evaluators have been treating
MMLU as a sanity check rather than a discriminator since at least
[Bowman's 2025 retrospective](https://example.com/bowman-2025), and
[the LMArena leaderboard](https://example.com/lmarena) is now where the
inter-vendor ordering is actually contested. Worth flagging when a
press release leads with an MMLU number — it's a signal that the
underlying release does not have a stronger claim to make.

## SWE-bench Verified refresh shifted the vendor ordering by ~7 points

The
[April 2026 refresh of SWE-bench Verified](https://example.com/swebench-2026)
added 142 issues from repos selected for under-representation in the
original split (database, devops, security tooling). On the new set,
Anthropic's Opus 4.7 drops from 71.8% (vendor-reported on the old
split) to 64.3% (third-party, new split). OpenAI's GPT-5.1 mainline
moves the other direction: 62.4% → 65.9%. xAI Grok-4-coder is roughly
flat. Re-running the new split on the old top-of-leaderboard models
moves nobody in the same direction — these are real composition shifts,
not noise.

The agentic-economy implication: the benchmarks that "AI replaces SWE"
discourse leans on are not stable bases. A 7-point reordering inside
the top three frontier models, on the same nominal benchmark, in a
single quarter, makes the "% of tasks AI can do" framing — popular in
the labor-economics literature on AI exposure — look fragile. Anyone
citing SWE-bench numbers from before the refresh as evidence of
labor-market displacement is now citing stale evidence.
[Acemoglu and Restrepo's 2025 update](https://example.com/acemoglu-2025)
already flagged the eval-stability issue; the refresh is the empirical
demonstration.

**Sources:** [SWE-bench April 2026 refresh notes](https://example.com/swebench-2026) · [Acemoglu & Restrepo 2025, "Eval drift and AI exposure"](https://example.com/acemoglu-2025) · [Anthropic model card update](https://example.com/anthropic-update) · [OpenAI changelog](https://example.com/openai-changelog)

## Quietly: RTVA Article 23 amendment in consultation through May 12

Switzerland's Federal Department of Communications opened consultation
on an amendment to
[Article 23 of the Federal Act on Radio and Television (RTVA)](https://example.com/rtva-art23)
that extends the public-broadcaster cross-financing prohibition to
adjacent digital products. The proposed text would prohibit SRG SSR
from using licence-fee revenue to subsidise online-only video and
podcast offerings outside the broadcasting mandate.

The incidence is non-obvious. Direct effect: SRG SSR's digital
expansion gets reined in. Indirect effect: the same revenue can
plausibly be redirected to commercial-broadcaster digital offerings
through the existing carve-outs in
[Article 38](https://example.com/rtva-art38), which the amendment
leaves untouched. The
[2024 Bundesrat report on private-broadcaster funding](https://example.com/bund-2024)
estimated those carve-outs at CHF 81m/year; the proposed amendment
does not adjust the cap. This is the structure E. Rentier flags as
classical regulatory rent: a public-interest framing on the headline,
incumbent benefit through an unmodified side channel.

Consultation closes May 12. The English translation has not been
posted; the
[official German text](https://example.com/rtva-de) and
[French version](https://example.com/rtva-fr) are on Fedlex.

**Sources:** [Federal Act on Radio and Television (RTVA)](https://example.com/rtva) · [2024 Bundesrat report on private-broadcaster funding](https://example.com/bund-2024) · [Consultation page on Fedlex](https://example.com/rtva-consultation)
