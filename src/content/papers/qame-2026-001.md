abstract: "This paper develops a formal axiomatic framework for analyzing rational reporter behavior when journalists face constraints on source access, verification costs, and editorial incentives. We derive equilibrium reporting strategies under different market structures and show how source competition affects truth revelation in media markets. The framework yields testable predictions about selective reporting, source capture, and the conditions under which competitive media markets produce more or less accurate information."
