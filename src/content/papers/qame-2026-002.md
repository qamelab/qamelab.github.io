abstract: "We propose a principal-agent framework for analysing multi-agent LLM hierarchies, drawing on classical contract theory to characterise moral hazard, adverse selection, and delegation costs when human principals instruct AI agents. Using a custom Python harness, we run controlled delegation experiments across leading frontier models and document systematic patterns in how agentic systems respond to incentive structures. We discuss implications for the design of agentic firms and for mechanism design in human–AI organisations."
